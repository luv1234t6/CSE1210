The Oklahoma City Thunder


About

Founded: 1967 as the Seattle SuperSonics, the team moved to Oklahoma City in 2008 and became the Oklahoma City Thunder. They currently play their home games at the Paycom Center in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma.

Championships and Success

The Thunder have won 0 NBA championships, but they have had notable success in the playoffs, including reaching the NBA Finals in 2012. The team has a rich history dating back to their time as the Seattle SuperSonics, with multiple playoff appearances and strong individual performances.

Thunder Legends

  • Kevin Durant: One of the best scorers in NBA history, Durant played for the Thunder from 2008 to 2016. He won the NBA MVP in 2014 and helped the team reach the NBA Finals in 2012.
  • Russell Westbrook: Known for his triple-double achievements, Westbrook played for the Thunder from 2008 to 2019. He won the NBA MVP in 2017 and set a record for the most triple-doubles in a season.
  • James Harden: A former member of the Thunder, Harden played alongside Durant and Westbrook from 2009 to 2012, before becoming a star in Houston. While with the Thunder, he won the NBA Sixth Man of the Year Award in 2012.
