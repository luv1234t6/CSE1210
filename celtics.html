The Boston Celtics


About

Founded: 1946, the Celtics are one of the oldest NBA teams, originally part of the Basketball Association of America (BAA), which later merged with the National Basketball League (NBL) to form the NBA. Location: Boston, Massachusetts, playing their home games at the TD Garden, which they share with the Boston Bruins of the NHL.

Championships and Success

The Celtics have won 18 NBA championships, tied with the Los Angeles Lakers for the most in league history. Their titles are spread across different eras, with their most dominant period being in the 1950s and 1960s.

Celtics Legends

  • Bill Russell: One of the greatest basketball players of all time, Russell played for the Celtics from 1956 to 1969 and won 11 championships in 13 seasons. He was known for his incredible defense, shot-blocking, and leadership.
  • Larry Bird: A three-time NBA MVP (1984-1986) and one of the most iconic players in NBA history, Bird led the Celtics to three championships (1981, 1984, 1986). His fierce rivalry with Magic Johnson, especially during the 1980s, helped elevate the league's popularity.
  • Paul Pierce: Known as "The Truth," Pierce was a key figure in the Celtics' 2008 championship win. He was a versatile scorer and clutch performer, earning an NBA Finals MVP in 2008.
